We compare three knowledge-source conditions under identical model, prompt, and task settings, each executed in 10 independent trials on a Si nMOSFET (\texttt{.cmd} Physics block + \texttt{.par} generation): (i) no external grounding (\texttt{no\_source}), (ii) a raw-text dump of Sentaurus manual excerpts (SDevice manual), and (iii) the ontology accessed through the four tools of Chapter III.B (\texttt{ontology\_mcp}, the PhysAgent configuration). Decks are evaluated by (a) \texttt{sdevice} parser acceptance (structural validity; convergence and IV match are out of scope) and (b) coverage of 14 canonical \texttt{.par} sections (e.g., Bandgap, Epsilon, ConstantMobility). \texttt{no\_source} hallucinates nonexistent keywords (0/10 pass, 39\% coverage); SDevice manual occupies $\sim$88\% of the 200K context window and improves coverage to 54\% but still fails every parser check (0/10), as the LLM cannot reliably lift structural keywords from prose (Fig.~\ref{fig:exp1}). Only \texttt{ontology\_mcp} achieves both parser pass and highest coverage (10/10, 81\%), through per-model SDevice syntax templates (\texttt{ontology\_get\_syntax}), equation-anchored literature values (\texttt{ontology\_get\_reference}, Fig.~\ref{fig:exp2}), and an explicit section checklist (\texttt{ontology\_overview}). The \textbf{structure---not the volume---of context} sets the quality ceiling in LLM-driven TCAD deck generation.
