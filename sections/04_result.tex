We compare three knowledge-source conditions under identical model ({\color{red}Anthropic \texttt{claude-sonnet-4-6}}) and prompt ({\color{red}\textit{``I want to simulate a silicon transistor in Sentaurus at 300\,K. Produce the Physics block of the cmd file and the corresponding .par file.''}}), each executed in 10 independent trials on a Si nMOSFET (\texttt{.cmd} Physics block + \texttt{.par} generation): (i) no external grounding (\texttt{no\_source}), (ii) a raw-text dump of Sentaurus manual excerpts ({\color{red}\texttt{SDevice manual}}), and (iii) the ontology accessed through the four tools of {\color{red}Chapter II.B} (\texttt{ontology\_mcp}, the PhysAgent configuration). Decks are evaluated by (a) {\color{red}successful loading of the generated deck by the \texttt{sdevice} engine (\textbf{pass/fail} per trial; failure types \textbf{K/V/M} detailed in Fig.~\ref{fig:exp1})} and (b) {\color{red}required-model mention rate --- the fraction of 14 required model keywords (e.g., \texttt{OldSlotboom}, \texttt{PhuMob}, \texttt{MultiValley}) that appear as case-insensitive substrings in the combined generated \texttt{.par}\,+\,Physics-block text}. \texttt{no\_source} hallucinates nonexistent keywords (0/10 pass, 39\% {\color{red}mention rate}); {\color{red}\texttt{SDevice manual}} {\color{red}contains all the required information, yet the LLM still produces incorrect syntax and fabricates parameter values from memory rather than the governing equations} --- {\color{red}mention rate rises to 54\%} but {\color{red}every trial still fails to load (0/10 pass)} (Fig.~\ref{fig:exp1}){\color{red}, motivating a structured ontology over raw prose}. Only \texttt{ontology\_mcp} achieves both {\color{red}successful loading} and highest {\color{red}mention rate} ({\color{red}10/10 pass}, 81\%) {\color{red}via a staged workflow: \texttt{ontology\_overview} maps the full structure (equation relations, physical meaning, parameter locations); \texttt{ontology\_get\_chapter} then selects, from the prompt's target material, the \texttt{.par} parameters and \texttt{.cmd} physical models grounded in the governing equations; \texttt{ontology\_get\_reference} supplies the matching literature values (Fig.~\ref{fig:exp2}); and \texttt{ontology\_get\_syntax} renders the final SDevice-compliant output}. The \textbf{structure---not the volume---of context} sets the quality ceiling in LLM-driven TCAD deck generation.
