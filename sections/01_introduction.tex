Recent LLM-based TCAD approaches rely on supervised fine-tuning (SFT) with synthetic datasets, focusing primarily on SDE structure generation. However, publicly available TCAD data is scarce, and physics model selection remains implicit. Rather than fine-tuning domain-specific models, we argue that building a knowledge structure for general-purpose LLMs is more effective. {\color{red}Table~\ref{tab:comparison}} summarizes the key differences.

\begin{table}[htbp]
\caption{Comparison of SFT-based approaches and PhysAgent.}
\label{tab:comparison}
\centering
\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.15}
\begin{tabular}{@{}lcc@{}}
\toprule
 & \textbf{SFT-based} & \textbf{PhysAgent} \\
\midrule
Training data required     & Large synthetic  & None \\
Target scope               & SDE structure    & SDevice physics \\
Physics model selection    & Implicit / fixed & Explicit (ontology) \\
Material-specific knowledge & Not addressed   & Literature-driven \\
LLM dependency             & Domain-specific  & General-purpose \\
Knowledge updatability     & Retrain needed   & Ontology update \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

We propose PhysAgent, which combines a Sentaurus TCAD guide-based ontology with literature-driven domain knowledge to generate physically grounded SDevice files for specific semiconductor materials.
