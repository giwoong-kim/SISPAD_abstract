TCAD simulation involves two main components: SDE for device geometry and meshing, and SDevice for physics models, boundary conditions, and solver settings. While SDE code generation has been widely explored with LLMs, SDevice configuration---selecting and parameterizing physics models such as mobility, recombination, and bandgap narrowing for specific materials---remains largely unaddressed due to its high domain expertise requirements.

LLMs have shown strong code generation capabilities in EDA tasks including RTL synthesis and circuit optimization. In TCAD, existing works have applied fine-tuned LLMs or in-context learning to generate structural code, but none systematically address physics-aware SDevice configuration grounded in explicit domain knowledge.
