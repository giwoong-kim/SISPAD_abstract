As semiconductor scaling approaches its limits, the importance of DTCO and TCAD continues to grow. However, TCAD automation requires more than simple code generation; it demands physically reliable Sdevice parameter generation and modeling. Existing SFT-based approaches mainly rely on open-source LLMs such as LLaMA, but they are inherently limited by the scarcity of publicly available TCAD data. Meanwhile, high-performance general-purpose LLMs such as GPT and Claude are rapidly advancing. Accordingly, the key is not to repeatedly fine-tune separate models, but to build a knowledge structure that enables these models to operate correctly in the TCAD context. To this end, we propose PhysAgent, which combines ontology-based TCAD knowledge with literature-driven domain knowledge.
